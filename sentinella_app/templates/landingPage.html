Security Overview

Theft Detection
in Supermarkets

Theft detection in supermarkets refers to the use of technology and security strategies to identify, prevent, and reduce shoplifting, employee theft, and inventory loss. Retail theft is one of the major causes of inventory shrinkage, which directly affects profitability.

Types of Theft

Shoplifting

Customers hiding products and leaving without paying.

Employee Theft

Staff stealing products or manipulating billing systems.

Barcode Switching

Replacing product barcodes with cheaper ones to pay significantly less than the item's actual value.

Sweethearting

Cashiers intentionally avoiding scanning certain items for friends, family, or accomplices at the checkout.

Return Fraud

Returning stolen, used, or counterfeit items for cash, store credit, or an exchange.

Detection Technologies

01

CCTV Surveillance

  • High-definition cameras monitor aisles, billing counters, and entrances.
  • AI-powered cameras detect suspicious behavior like hiding items.
02

RFID

Radio Frequency Identification

  • Products contain specific tracking tags.
  • Sensors at exit gates automatically trigger alarms if unpaid items pass through.
03

EAS Systems

Electronic Article Surveillance

  • Physical security tags firmly attached to products.
  • Dedicated alarm systems at store exits detect unpaid items instantly.
04

AI & Machine Learning

  • Behavior analysis using computer vision.
  • Detects unusual movements or repeated suspicious actions.
  • Can identify patterns in billing data to detect employee fraud.
05

Smart POS Monitoring

  • Monitors unusual, rapid, or irregular transactions at checkout.
  • Automatically flags suspicious refunds, zero-dollar transactions, or excessive price overrides.
